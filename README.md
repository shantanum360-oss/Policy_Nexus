# Policy Nexus

A data-driven public policy research project focused on evaluating the impact of the **Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)** on financial risk protection and healthcare outcomes. The project integrates econometric methods and machine learning techniques to generate evidence-based policy recommendations.

## Overview

Policy Nexus is a research initiative undertaken under the **Public Policy and Opinion Cell, IIT Kanpur**, aimed at assessing the effectiveness of PM-JAY in reducing healthcare-related financial vulnerability among Indian households.

The project leverages large-scale national survey datasets, including **NSSO** and **NFHS**, to conduct rigorous quantitative analyses and derive actionable policy insights.

## Objectives

* Evaluate the impact of PM-JAY on financial risk protection among households.
* Analyze socio-economic disparities in healthcare vulnerability.
* Assess PM-JAY outcomes using econometric and machine learning techniques.
* Generate evidence-based recommendations for policymakers.

## Methodology

### Data Sources

The analysis utilizes nationally representative datasets:

* National Sample Survey Office (NSSO)
* National Family Health Survey (NFHS)

### Econometric Analysis

Applied the following econometric techniques:

* Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) Regression
* Difference-in-Differences (DiD) Estimation

These methods were employed to estimate the causal impact of PM-JAY on healthcare expenditure and financial protection.

### Machine Learning Analysis

Implemented machine learning models to identify vulnerable population groups:

* Decision Trees
* Classification-based vulnerability profiling

### Data Processing

Performed:

* Data cleaning and preprocessing
* Feature engineering
* Statistical analysis
* Visualization and interpretation of results
